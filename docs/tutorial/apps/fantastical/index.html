Fantastical Module Overview

The Fantastical module provides access to Fantastical’s features from within PyXA, making use of both the application’s scripting dictionary as well as its URL scheme.

Examples

Example 1 - Interacting with Currently Selected Calendar Items

1 import PyXA
2 app = PyXA.application("Fantastical")
3 items = app.selected_calendar_items()
4 urls = [item.show_url.url for item in items]
5 print(urls)
6 # ['x-fantastical://show?item=7d627e52-ae3d-39eb-b86b-57b037f92cab&calendarIdentifier=A5E06B53-667F-42EE-A6FD-99609F6711E3&date=2022-02-21%2000:00', 'x-fantastical://show?item=e4bcc8c4-cd34-3c1d-b273-def4ecd47eae&calendarIdentifier=A5E06B53-667F-42EE-A6FD-99609F6711E3&date=2022-02-14%2000:00', ...]

Example 2 - Using Natural Language to Create Events

1 import PyXA
2 app = PyXA.application("Fantastical")
3 app.parse_sentence("Meeting from 2pm to 3 today")
4 app.parse_sentence("Joe's birthday August 26th all day")
5 app.parse_sentence("Computer Science Homework due on Tuesday")
6 app.parse_sentence("Vacation from August 26 to September 2")
7 app.parse_sentence("Meeting at 2pm today alert 20 minutes before")
8 app.parse_sentence("Attend Apple Event at 1 Infinite Loop, Cupertino, CA on September 14 at 9am")
9 app.parse_sentence("Remind me to clean on Wednesday")
